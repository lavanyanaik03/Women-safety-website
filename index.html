Self-Defense Tips

These tips are for awareness only. Your safety is the first priority.

1. Stay aware of your surroundings

Avoid walking with full volume earphones in lonely areas, keep your phone charged, and notice exits, people, and lighting around you.

2. Trust your instincts

If a situation or person feels unsafe, move away as soon as possible. Do not worry about being “polite” in that moment.

3. Keep your emergency tools ready

Keep your phone accessible, SOS site open, and if legal in your area, carry a simple self-defense tool like a whistle.

4. Use your voice

Shouting loudly for help, saying “No, stop” confidently can alert people nearby and discourage an attacker.

5. Basic physical defense

Target sensitive areas: eyes, nose, throat, groin, knees. Use the heel of your palm, your knee, or your elbow. Take a professional self-defense class if possible for proper training.
